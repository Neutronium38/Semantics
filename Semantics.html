Characteristics Kanibo has two faces on its back and stomach resembling "oni (鬼)", a kind of demon in Japanese folklore. Its body is mainly a deep red color. It has six legs, with a tan upper part and a red lower part. Each leg ends with two claws. Like Kanki, Kanibo has a metal club-shaped claw, but upon evolution, it gains another one on its left arm. There are three pairs of spikes on its head, with two pairs on its head and another one closer to its back. It has a tan-colored jaw with yellow eyes.
Origin Kanibo's design and Loomipedia entry likely refer to the heikegani (平家蟹), a species of crab whose shells resemble the faces of samurai and said in Japanese legend to have been the reincarnations of Heike (平家) samurai. Its design likely takes inspiration from Japanese oni (鬼), a troll or demon typically depicted as wielding a large kanabō (金棒). Kanibo is a portmanteau of the words "kani (蟹)", Japanese for crab, and "kanabō (金棒)", a spiked, two-handed samurai war club, referring to its spiky club-shaped pincers.
